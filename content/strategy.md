## Channels + cadence

| Channel | Author | Frequency | Goal | Format |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Founder X / Twitter | _name_ | Daily | Reach + trust | One specific post per day |
| Company blog | rotating | 2 / month | SEO + depth | 1500–3000 words, charts |
| LinkedIn | _name_ | 2 / week | Mid-market reach | Slightly more accessible than X |
| 小红书 (if applicable) | _name_ | 1 / week | China founder audience | Multi-slide story |

## Pillars (3–5 topics we own)

| Pillar | Why it's ours | Example post |
|---|---|---|
| _e.g. AI-native ICP definition_ | Specific framework we built | "Three ICP axes traditional SaaS doesn't have" |
| _e.g. Death cases_ | Original reporting | "What Jasper actually got wrong" |
| _e.g. MCP / agent distribution_ | Early to the topic | "Why your product needs an MCP server in 2026" |

Sticking to these makes us recognizable. Drifting into "thoughts on hiring" or "founder reflections" dilutes positioning unless that's pillar 4.

---

## Anti-patterns

- "Excited to announce" intros
- LinkedIn humble-brag thread format
- Listicles without a thesis
- Generic AI commentary that any of 50 newsletters cover
- Posts without a number, a chart, a code sample, or a name

---

## Linking back to product

Every post should have one of:
- Link to a specific file in this repo
- Link to a customer case (with permission)
- Link to a tool / playground / live demo
- Link to a research source (not just our own opinion)

If a post has zero outbound links, it's vibes content — fix or kill.
