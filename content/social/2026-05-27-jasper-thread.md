# Jasper Thread · X / Twitter

## Tweet 1 (hook)

Oct 2022: Jasper raises $125M at $1.5B. Projects $250M ARR by 2024.

Sep 2023: Both founders resign. ARR projection cut from $90M to ~$55M.

Here's the post-mortem nobody wrote — and 3 things every wrapper company should learn from it. 🧵

## Tweet 2

Jasper's product in 2022 was 4 layers:

1. Wrapper around OpenAI's GPT-3 API
2. Library of prompt templates
3. UI + brand voice tooling
4. Team/billing on top

Layer 1 was rented. Layer 4 was Stripe. Their moat lived in 2 and 3.

## Tweet 3

Nov 30, 2022: ChatGPT launches. Free.

Every Jasper user discovered they could open ChatGPT, type "write a Facebook ad", and get 80% of Jasper's output without paying $49/mo.

Six months later GPT-3.5 shipped via API. The 20% gap shrank further.

## Tweet 4

What Jasper did, eventually:
- Repositioned to "marketing teams at mid-market" (left individual creator behind)
- Built a brand-voice fine-tuning layer
- Shipped integrations (Surfer SEO, Google Docs)

These moves saved the company at a much smaller valuation.

## Tweet 5

What they should have done in late 2022:

1. Re-baseline ICP within 30 days of ChatGPT — kill the SMB tier, double down on Teams
2. Pre-announce the pivot before revenue collapsed (own the narrative)
3. Use pricing as a weapon, not a defense — drop entry tier or consolidate to $99+

## Tweet 6

Three things every wrapper company should do in 2026:

1. Write your capability-obsolescence radar — list expected model releases + self-assess each
2. Invest in workflow/data/integration layer NOW (the layer foundation models won't replicate)
3. Decide your "pivot trigger" date in advance — pivot before metrics deteriorate

Includes Inflection ($4B → Microsoft non-acquisition), Character.AI ($5B → $2.7B reverse acqui-hire), the entire PDF-Chat wave.
