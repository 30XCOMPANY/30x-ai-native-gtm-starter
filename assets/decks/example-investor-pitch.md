# Deck · Investor Pitch (Series A)

- Audience: Series A investors (a16z / Sequoia / OpenAI Fund tier)
- Purpose: raise capital
- Duration: 20 min (12 slides)
- Theme: orange-deep accent on paper (per `assets/deck-style.md`)
- Generated by: `skills/deck-build` (example output)

> Status: **example only**. Adapt to your company, then re-run `skills/deck-build`.

## Slide 1 · Cover

- **Master**: cover
- **Eyebrow**: SERIES A · 2026
- **Title**: [Company]. The [category] for [target]. *italic accent*
- **Meta**: [Audience name] · [Date] · Confidential
- **Visual**: logo bottom-left, page 1/12 bottom-right
- **Speaker note**: Hold for 2 beats. Set up the room. Don't read the slide.

## Slide 2 · The Why-Now

- **Master**: stat
- **Big number**: 6 months
- **Label**: AI MARKET REFRESH CYCLE
- **Context**: "Foundation models reshuffle the market every 6 months. The companies that adapt at that cadence win. Most don't."
- **Speaker note**: This is the opening hook. Sets up that we're in a uniquely fast market.

## Slide 3 · The Problem

- **Master**: headline + body
- **Title**: Your AI startup's GTM has the same fault line.
- **Body** (3 bullets max):
  - Static ICPs rot in 30 days when the model changes
  - Sales playbooks built for SaaS funnel break for PLG + integration
  - Capability obsolescence killed Jasper (-40% ARR), Inflection, Adept, Character.AI in 18 months
- **Speaker note**: The audience knows these names. Don't sell them on the problem; trigger their existing pattern recognition.

## Slide 4 · The Solution (in one sentence)

- **Master**: headline + body
- **Title**: We are the GTM operating system for AI-native companies.
- **Body** (1 paragraph): [Your one-sentence elevator pitch + key technical differentiator. Max 30 words.]
- **Visual**: minimal diagram showing your product fitting into customer's workflow
- **Speaker note**: This is the slide they'll quote later. Make it portable.

## Slide 5 · How it works (1 of 2)

- **Master**: diagram
- **Visual**: your core product diagram — max 8 elements, no gradients
- **Caption** (≤ 12 words): "What [your customer's role] uses, every day."
- **Speaker note**: Walk through the workflow with the audience in their seat. Where do they grunt with recognition? That's your conversion-driving moment.

## Slide 6 · How it works (2 of 2)

- **Master**: diagram
- **Visual**: the technical-credibility-ladder slide — show one proof artifact (benchmark / code sample / latency)
- **Caption**: "Why an engineer trusts it in 5 minutes."
- **Speaker note**: This is where you transition from product to traction.

## Slide 7 · Traction

- **Master**: stat (use multiple)
- **Stats** (4 boxes):
  - $X ARR (or $X user spend on platform)
  - X% MoM growth
  - X customers (logo grid optional, only if real)
  - X engineering integrations shipped
- **Speaker note**: One number per box. Investors compute the ratios.

## Slide 8 · Why it's defensible

- **Master**: headline + body
- **Title**: Defensible because [unique thing — data network effect / integration depth / compliance ownership / community].
- **Body**: 3 bullets explaining the moat. Reference `strategy/capability-radar.md` self-assessment if available.
- **Speaker note**: Investors will push on "but what if GPT-6 ships [your feature]?" — have the response ready. Don't dodge.

## Slide 9 · Market

- **Master**: headline + body
- **Title**: The market is [TAM number] and growing because [why].
- **Body** (3 bullets): TAM build-up — be honest about how you arrive at the number.
- **Speaker note**: Don't over-claim TAM. A defensible $X billion is better than an indefensible $XX billion.

## Slide 10 · Team

- **Master**: headline + body (with people photos)
- **Title**: Built by people who've seen this from inside.
- **Body** (per founder): name + role + previous company / unique credibility
- **Speaker note**: This is where investors decide whether to back you. Don't oversell — your most credible factual story wins.

## Slide 11 · Use of funds

- **Master**: stat slide
- **Title**: We're raising $X to do Y.
- **Stats**: 3 allocations (e.g., 50% eng, 30% GTM, 20% reserves)
- **Body**: 1 sentence each on what the bucket delivers
- **Speaker note**: This converts the conversation from "are you good?" to "is this a good deal?"

## Slide 12 · CTA

- **Master**: cta close
- **Title**: We close the round in [N] weeks. *italic accent*
- **Single ask**: "If you're in, send a term sheet by [date]. If you're out, tell us fast — we have other meetings."
- **Contact**: [founder name] · [email]
- **Speaker note**: Don't blink. Investors hate softness on the close. State your timeline like you mean it.

## Quality check

- [x] Cover + CTA bookend
- [x] 1 stat slide + 1 diagram slide (have 2 of each)
- [x] ≤30 words per body slide
- [x] ≤3 colors per slide (orange-deep, ink, paper)
- [x] No "Excited to announce", "best-in-class", "AI-powered"
- [x] Single CTA on close slide
- [x] All claims should have backing in `strategy/` or be replaced with placeholders

## Common failures to avoid

- 30 slides for a 20-min meeting — kill 15
- Logo wall on slide 2 when you have 3 customers
- "Competition" 2x2 quadrant with you in the upper-right corner
- Pricing buried on slide 11 when investors will ask by slide 5
- "Thank you / Q&A" closing slide — yours ends on the ASK
